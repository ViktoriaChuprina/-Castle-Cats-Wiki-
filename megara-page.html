The facts

Megara is possibly a reference to the first wife of Heracles and firstborn Theban princess, Megara, from Greek mythology.

  • The claim in her Hero Book entry that she is "part of a royal griffin family" and her line, "My father is very important," would then be references to Megara's father Creon, the King of Thebes.
  • Megara might jointly be a reference to the mythical winged horse Pegasus.
  • Megara the griffin is a flying cat, and Pegasus is a flying horse.
  • Heracles is often, albeit incorrectly, depicted as a friend and rider of Pegasus, such as in Disney's 1997 film, Hercules.
  • Her line, "I fly wherever I want," could be an obscure reference to the hero Bellerophon, who (in one version of his myth) attempts to ride Pegasus up to Mount Olympus until Zeus has him thrown off, as Mount Olympus is the home of the gods.
